## How to Use This Template

This is a three-part instrument: pre-play demographics, during-play observation, and post-play feedback. The facilitator fills in Part 2 during the session. The playtester fills in Parts 1 and 3.

**Facilitator responsibilities:**
- Print or share Part 1 before the session starts
- Observe silently during play -- do not help, hint, or explain unless the tester is stuck for more than 2 minutes on something that is not the subject of the test
- Timestamp observations in Part 2
- Hand off Part 3 immediately after the tester puts down the controller -- do not let them "think about it later"

**Sample size guidance:**
- 5 testers reveal ~85% of usability issues (Nielsen, 2000)
- 8-12 testers provide reliable quantitative signal on Likert scales
- 20+ testers required for statistically significant NPS data

**Reference**: Nintendo's internal playtest methodology isolates testers behind one-way mirrors with zero facilitator intervention. Larian Studios ran over 1,000 community feedback sessions during Baldur's Gate 3 early access, structured around specific hypothesis testing per patch.

### 2.1 Session Log

Record observations as they happen. Timestamp everything. Do not interpret -- describe what you see.

**Coding Key:**
- **[C]** Confusion -- tester pauses, looks lost, re-reads UI
- **[F]** Frustration -- visible annoyance, repeated failure, sighing, muttering
- **[D]** Delight -- smile, laugh, verbal positive reaction, leaning forward
- **[S]** Stuck -- tester cannot progress, tries the same thing repeatedly
- **[E]** Exploration -- tester voluntarily investigates something off the main path
- **[I]** Ignore -- tester skips or misses content/UI the design expects them to engage with

## Part 4: Hypothesis Testing (Customize Per Session)

Each playtest should test 1-3 specific hypotheses. Define them before the session. Evaluate them after.

### Hypothesis Template

| # | Hypothesis | Metric / Observable | Result | Verdict |
|---|-----------|-------------------|--------|---------|
| H1 | [Statement to test, e.g., "Players will discover the wall-jump mechanic within the first 5 minutes without explicit instruction"] | [How you will measure: observation code, survey question, time-to-completion] | [Post-session: what actually happened] | [ ] Confirmed / [ ] Partially Confirmed / [ ] Rejected |
| H2 | [Hypothesis] | [Metric] | [Result] | [ ] Confirmed / [ ] Partially / [ ] Rejected |
| H3 | [Hypothesis] | [Metric] | [Result] | [ ] Confirmed / [ ] Partially / [ ] Rejected |

### Hypothesis Writing Guidelines

Good hypotheses are:
- **Specific**: "Players will complete Level 2 in under 8 minutes" not "Level 2 is fun"
- **Measurable**: Tied to an observable behavior or a survey response
- **Falsifiable**: It is possible to get a result that clearly rejects the hypothesis
- **Actionable**: If rejected, you know what to change

**Example hypotheses by development phase:**

| Phase | Example Hypothesis |
|-------|-------------------|
| Prototype | "The core loop (fight-loot-upgrade) is engaging with placeholder art" |
| Vertical Slice | "Players understand the crafting system without reading the tutorial text" |
| Alpha | "The difficulty curve from Zone 1 to Zone 2 does not cause a drop-off spike" |
| Beta | "Players rate the final boss fight above 4.0 on the satisfaction scale" |
| Pre-Release | "NPS score is above 40 across all tester demographics" |
